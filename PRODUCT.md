# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Restaurant owners and managers evaluating a premium website service, plus diners who scan a QR code and browse the mobile menu at the table.

## Product Purpose

The project demonstrates a modern restaurant landing page, a mobile-first visual menu with short dish videos, and an admin panel for managing restaurant content. Success means the pilot feels valuable enough that a restaurant owner can immediately imagine paying for the service.

## Brand Personality

Cinematic, bold, appetizing. The experience should feel crafted, confident, and memorable without hiding the food behind generic decoration.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic template landing pages, beige craft-brand defaults, flat card grids, weak button feedback, stock-looking food presentation, and heavy animations that make ordering feel slow.

## Design Principles

Show the food first.
Make the mobile menu feel like the main product, not an afterthought.
Use motion to create appetite and clarity, not delay.
Preserve the restaurant's practical information and ordering flow.
Make every first screen feel presentation-ready for a client demo.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast, readable mobile type, obvious touch targets, and reduced-motion alternatives for major transitions and ambient effects.
